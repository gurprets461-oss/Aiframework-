# AIFramework.site

**AIFramework.site** is a polished public-facing research platform examining fairness, demographic transparency, and accountability in FDA-approved and FDA-cleared AI diagnostic tools.

The site translates a complex policy and medical AI issue into an accessible, evidence-driven resource for students, researchers, clinicians, policymakers, hospitals, and the general public.

## Overview

AIFramework.site focuses on a critical question in medical AI oversight:

**Do public-facing regulatory records provide enough information to evaluate whether AI diagnostic tools were tested fairly across diverse populations?**

Rather than simply asking whether medical AI is biased, the project investigates whether the available documentation makes fairness risk visible in the first place.

The site presents this issue through a structured research narrative, combining plain-language explanations, evidence summaries, policy recommendations, an auditing framework, and illustrative case studies.

## Key Features

### Public Research Platform

The website is designed as a complete public resource on fairness in medical AI. It explains how AI tools are used in healthcare, why demographic representation matters, and why regulatory transparency is essential for responsible deployment.

### Evidence-Driven Findings

The project highlights major transparency gaps in public-facing documentation for AI-enabled medical devices, including limited reporting of race, ethnicity, age, socioeconomic data, subgroup performance, and post-market monitoring.

### Policy Brief

The site includes a policy-focused section arguing for stronger minimum disclosure standards in FDA-facing documentation. Recommendations include clearer demographic reporting, validation population descriptions, subgroup performance visibility, and post-market monitoring expectations.

### Auditing Framework

A central feature of the project is the **Demographic Transparency and Deployment Readiness Rubric**. This framework helps reviewers assess whether public records contain enough fairness-relevant information to evaluate an AI diagnostic or triage tool responsibly.

The rubric evaluates criteria such as:

- Intended use clarity
- Training population transparency
- Validation population detail
- Race and ethnicity reporting
- Age reporting
- Sex or gender reporting
- Multisite validation
- Subgroup performance reporting
- Real-world evaluation
- Post-market monitoring
- Workflow limitations
- Local deployment fit

### Case Studies

The site applies the framework to several FDA-authorized AI medical devices, including:

- IDx-DR
- ContaCT / Viz.ai
- GI Genius
- Rapid ICH
- AEYE-DS

These case studies illustrate how public-facing documentation can vary widely in transparency and usefulness for fairness review.

### Public Guide

AIFramework.site also includes a practical public guide titled **“What to Ask About Medical AI.”** This section turns the research into clear questions that patients, students, and nontechnical readers can use to think critically about medical AI.

## Why It Matters

AI-enabled diagnostic and triage tools are increasingly used in real healthcare settings. These tools can influence screening, prioritization, alerts, diagnostic support, and clinical workflow.

However, strong overall performance does not guarantee equitable performance across all patient groups. A tool may appear accurate on average while performing less reliably for certain populations.

AIFramework.site makes the case that transparency is a prerequisite for accountability. Without clear demographic and subgroup information, hospitals, researchers, and the public cannot easily determine whether fairness risks were adequately assessed.
